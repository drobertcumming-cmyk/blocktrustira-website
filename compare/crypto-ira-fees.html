Per-Trade Fees vs. Management Fees

The cheapest option is not always the best option. The real question is what you are paying for.

Per-Trade Fee Model

You pay a percentage each time you buy or sell. The provider earns revenue from your trading volume. This model works well for self-directed investors who trade infrequently.

Who uses it: iTrustCapital, BitcoinIRA, BlockTrustIRA Self-Directed

Consideration: The provider profits from your activity, not your results. More trades mean more revenue for them, regardless of your portfolio performance.

Management + Performance Model

You pay a base management fee plus a share of profits. The provider earns more when your portfolio grows. This model is standard in institutional asset management.

Who uses it: BlockTrustIRA Managed IRA

Consideration: The provider is financially motivated to grow your account. If the portfolio does not grow, the performance fee is zero. Incentives are aligned.

"The cheapest option isn't always the best option. The question is what you're paying for."

A per-trade model charges you for every decision. A performance model charges you for results. Neither is inherently better. The right choice depends on your investment approach and how much active management you want.

Self-directed investors who trade rarely may pay very little in per-trade fees. Investors who want professional management should evaluate whether the management fee is justified by the service delivered.

What to Watch For

Fees That Are Easy to Miss

Not all costs appear on the fee schedule. Here are the common ones investors overlook.

Spread Markup

Some providers add a markup to the buy/sell price of each cryptocurrency. This functions as a hidden fee. It does not appear on your statement. The difference between the market price and your execution price is the cost.

Account Maintenance Fees

Monthly or annual account fees can erode returns over time. A $20/month fee costs $1,200 over five years. Check whether your provider charges these separately from trading fees.

Withdrawal and Transfer Fees

Moving your IRA to a different provider should not cost a significant amount. Some platforms charge transfer-out fees. Ask about these before you open an account.

Inactivity Fees

Some providers charge fees if you do not trade within a certain period. This creates a perverse incentive to trade more than necessary. Ask whether inactivity fees apply.
