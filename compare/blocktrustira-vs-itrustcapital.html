Key Differences

Where the Two Diverge

The comparison table shows the data. These sections explain what it means in practice.

Trading Fees: 0.14% vs. 1%

BlockTrustIRA's Managed IRA charges 0.14% per trade through sFOX. iTrustCapital charges 1% per trade. On a $10,000 trade, that is $14 versus $100.

The Self-Directed option at 0.4% per trade is still less than half of iTrustCapital's rate. For active traders, this difference compounds significantly over time.

Context: iTrustCapital's 1% fee is straightforward and all-inclusive. BlockTrustIRA's Managed IRA also includes management and performance fees. Compare total cost, not just per-trade rates.

Management: AI-Managed vs. Self-Directed Only

iTrustCapital provides a trading platform. You make every investment decision. There is no management, monitoring, or rebalancing service. This works well for experienced, engaged investors.

BlockTrustIRA offers the same self-directed capability. But it also offers an AI-managed option. The Animus AI system monitors your portfolio 24/7 and rebalances based on market conditions. This is a fundamentally different service.

Context: If you want a hands-off approach, iTrustCapital does not offer one. If you want full control, both providers accommodate that.

Fee Model: Performance-Aligned vs. Per-Trade

iTrustCapital earns revenue from your trading activity. Every trade generates a 1% fee regardless of whether the trade was profitable. The provider benefits from volume.

BlockTrustIRA's Managed IRA earns a base management fee plus 25% of profits. If your portfolio does not grow, the performance fee is zero. The provider benefits from your results.

Context: Neither model is inherently wrong. Per-trade fees are simple and predictable. Performance fees create alignment but add cost when returns are strong. Understand the incentive structure behind each model.

Custody Insurance: $200M Specified vs. Unspecified

BlockTrustIRA publishes a specific custody insurance figure: $200M through sFOX. This number is verifiable and represents the maximum coverage for assets under custody.

iTrustCapital does not prominently publish a specific custody insurance amount. This does not mean assets are uninsured. It means the information is not as readily available for comparison.

Context: Ask both providers directly about insurance coverage. A specific, published number makes due diligence easier. Absence of a published number is not evidence of absence.

The Core Framing

Platform vs. Portfolio Manager

This is the fundamental difference between these two providers. iTrustCapital is a platform. You get access, tools, and a clean interface. You are the pilot.

BlockTrustIRA is a platform and a portfolio manager. You can fly yourself. Or you can hand the controls to a system designed to watch the instruments around the clock.

"One gives you the cockpit. The other gives you a cockpit and a pilot. You choose which you need."

Neither approach is superior in the abstract. The right choice depends on how involved you want to be in managing your retirement portfolio on a daily basis.

Verdict

  • You want AI-managed portfolio monitoring
  • You prefer a performance-aligned fee structure
