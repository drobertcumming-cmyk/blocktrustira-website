Incentive Alignment

The Zero-Conflict Model

Most crypto IRA providers earn money when you trade. Buy or sell, win or lose, they collect a fee on every transaction. Their incentive is your activity, not your growth.

We built a different model. Our performance fee means we only earn more when your portfolio earns more. If your account doesn't grow, we don't collect a performance fee. Our financial interest and yours point in the same direction.

"We earn when you earn. That's the alignment."

Common Questions

Fee FAQ

The 2% annual management fee is calculated based on your total assets under management. It's applied proportionally throughout the year, not as a single lump-sum charge. If your account balance is $50,000, the annual fee is $1,000.

The performance fee applies only to profits. If your portfolio doesn't grow, you pay $0 in performance fees. We use a high-water mark, so you're never charged on gains that merely recover previous losses. This fee structure ensures our incentive is aligned with your portfolio growth.

The 0.14% trading fee is the execution cost per trade, charged by sFOX, the institutional exchange where your trades are executed. This is significantly lower than the 1-2% per-trade fees common on many crypto IRA platforms. We don't mark up this fee.

No. There is no setup fee, no account opening fee, and no closure fee. If you decide to leave, you can close your account and transfer your assets without penalty. There are no lock-up periods.

Most crypto IRA providers charge 1-2% per trade with no active management. Over a year of normal trading activity, those per-trade fees can exceed our total annual cost — and you receive no portfolio management, no rebalancing, and no AI monitoring in return. Our model costs less over time and delivers significantly more value.

The performance fee is proof of incentive alignment. If we only charged a flat management fee, our revenue would be the same whether your portfolio grew 30% or lost 10%. The performance fee ensures we're financially motivated to grow your portfolio. When you do well, we do well.

A high-water mark means we only charge the performance fee on new profits above the previous peak. If your portfolio reaches $60,000, then drops to $55,000, then recovers to $62,000, we only charge the performance fee on the $2,000 above the previous $60,000 high. You're never double-charged on recovery gains.
